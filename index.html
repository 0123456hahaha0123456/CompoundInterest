Total Contributions

The raw money you put in — your initial investment plus every monthly contribution added together over time. No growth or interest is included here. This is your "out of pocket" line.

Future Value

The projected balance of your account over time, including compound interest growth on top of your contributions. This is how much money the account would show on a statement at each year — the "nominal" dollar amount.

Future Value vs. Inflation

Compares three lines so you can see what your money is really worth: Total Contributions (what you put in), Future Value (the nominal balance — what the statement shows), and Real Value (the future balance converted back into today's purchasing power, i.e. what it would actually buy you in 2026 dollars after inflation). The gap between Future Value and Real Value is how much of your gain inflation quietly eats.
